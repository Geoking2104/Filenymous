🔒 Privacy & GDPR

End-to-end encryption

Files are encrypted with AES-256-GCM directly in your browser before any transfer takes place. The AES session key is itself encrypted via ECIES/X25519 (HKDF-SHA-256) using the recipient's X25519 public key. Nobody — not DHT nodes, not the Filenymous team — can decrypt your files.

Peer-to-peer Holochain network

There is no central server. Data is distributed across the Holochain DHT. Each participant hosts a fraction of the encrypted network. Your email or phone number is never published in plaintext — only its SHA-256 fingerprint (non-reversible) travels on the network.

Keys and identity

Your X25519 private key is generated locally and stored in your browser's IndexedDB. It never leaves your device. OTP verification is handled by a bridge service (Node.js + Fastify) that keeps no contact logs.

Retention period

Transfers expire according to the duration chosen at send time (24h, 7d, 30d, or no expiry). After expiry or when the download quota is reached, the manifest is revoked on the DHT and the encrypted chunks become inaccessible.

GDPR

Filenymous collects no personally identifiable data. No tracking cookies. No behavioral analytics. OTP notifications transit via SendGrid / Twilio — their policies apply for message delivery only. Addresses are not stored.
